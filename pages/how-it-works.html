Select Your Chain and Asset

Nine chains, forty-plus tokens.

BITVORA supports seven blockchains — Bitcoin, Ethereum, BNB Smart Chain, Tron, Solana, TON, and Litecoin. Each chain has a unique deposit address generated and maintained by the platform.

Selecting a chain filters the token list automatically — only tokens available on that specific chain appear in the selection. The selected chain and token pair determine which RPC verifier node processes your transaction on the backend.

The chain selector is the first decision in the flow and directly determines the deposit address you will use. Different chains for the same token — for example, USDT on Ethereum versus USDT on Tron — will have different addresses. Always verify the chain before sending.

02

Send to Our Deposit Address

Copy the address. Send from your own wallet.

After selecting your chain and token, a deposit address is displayed alongside a QR code for convenient mobile scanning. Send the exact amount you wish to convert from your self-custody wallet to this address.

Important warning: Sending from a centralized exchange like Binance or Coinbase can break TXID traceability. Centralized exchanges often batch multiple withdrawals into a single on-chain transaction, making it impossible to isolate your specific transfer. Always send from a self-custody wallet such as MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Phantom, or Ledger.

The amount sent does not need to match the quoted amount with perfect precision — there is a small tolerance window. However, rates are locked at the time of submission, and the INR payout is calculated based on the actual amount received on-chain, not the quoted amount.

03

Submit Your TXID

Paste your transaction hash. We verify on-chain.

A TXID — transaction ID — is a unique alphanumeric string that identifies your transaction on the blockchain. You can find it in your wallet's transaction history immediately after sending, or by searching your sending address on a blockchain explorer such as Etherscan, Tronscan, Solscan, or Blockstream.

The backend verification process calls blockchain RPC nodes directly. It checks that the recipient address matches our platform deposit address, verifies the exact amount received, and waits for the chain's required number of confirmations. This means there is no third-party block explorer dependency — the chain itself provides the verification.

A distributed lock mechanism prevents duplicate processing. Once a TXID is accepted into the system, a lock is acquired for that hash across all verifier threads, ensuring the same transaction cannot be processed twice even under concurrent submissions.

04

Receive INR

Enter your UPI or bank account. Funds arrive in minutes.

After on-chain verification completes, you enter your UPI ID or bank account number and IFSC code. The transaction moves to the payout queue and an admin processes the INR transfer manually via IMPS or UPI.

The typical timeline is: transaction detected within seconds of confirmation, verification completes in one to two minutes depending on chain, payout queued immediately after verification, and INR transferred within ten to twenty minutes. Total flow from confirmed transaction to INR received typically takes under fifteen minutes during business hours.

Every transaction generates a unique reference URL. You can track the exact status of your transaction in real time — from submitted to verified to payout initiated to completed. This status page remains permanently accessible via the reference URL so you always have a full audit trail.

A payout reference number is provided after the INR transfer completes. Keep this for your records — it corresponds to the IMPS or UPI transaction from our payment account to yours.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

What wallets are supported? +

Any self-custody wallet that lets you send transactions and shows you a TXID is supported. This includes MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Phantom, Exodus, Ledger, Trezor, Atomic Wallet, Coinomi, Electrum for Bitcoin, and any other wallet where you control your private keys. The key requirement is that you must be able to send to a specific address and retrieve the TXID afterward.

What happens if I send the wrong asset? +

Sending a different token than selected can cause your submission to fail verification, since the backend checks the specific token contract address. If this happens, contact support immediately with your TXID and the exact asset you sent. Recovery depends on the chain and asset involved — some misrouted tokens can be recovered, others cannot. Always triple-check the asset and chain before sending.

Can I send from Binance or Coinbase? +

This is not recommended and often breaks the process. Centralized exchanges batch withdrawals, meaning your transaction may be bundled with others into a single on-chain transfer. When this happens, the TXID belongs to a different originating address, making it impossible to match your specific transfer to our deposit address. Always withdraw to a self-custody wallet first, then send from there.

What if my transaction has zero confirmations? +

Transactions with zero confirmations are pending and unconfirmed. The backend will not process them until the minimum confirmation count for that chain is reached. You can submit your TXID immediately after sending — the system will detect the pending transaction and wait for the required confirmations automatically. You do not need to wait before submitting.

How long does verification take? +

Verification time depends entirely on the blockchain confirmation speed. Fast chains like Solana and TON confirm in seconds. Ethereum typically takes one to three minutes. Bitcoin takes the longest — one confirmation is approximately ten minutes. Once the minimum confirmation count is reached, our backend verifier processes the result within seconds. The total verification time is almost entirely driven by the chain's block time.

What if I close the browser during verification? +

Closing the browser does not affect verification. The backend processes your TXID independently of your browser session. Once you submit a TXID, the verification job runs server-side. You can return to your reference URL at any time to check the status. The page will reflect the current state of your transaction regardless of when you last had it open.

Can I cancel a transaction after submitting? +

Once a TXID is submitted and verification begins, cancellation is not possible on the platform side. The underlying on-chain transaction is already irreversible by definition of how blockchains work. If you need assistance with a transaction that was submitted in error, contact support with your reference URL. We can advise on next steps based on the specific situation.

What is the minimum and maximum amount? +

There is no enforced minimum or maximum on the platform. However, very small amounts may not be economical after the network fee cost is factored in. Very large amounts may trigger additional manual review steps before payout is released. For amounts above a certain threshold, reaching out to support in advance is recommended to ensure smooth processing.
